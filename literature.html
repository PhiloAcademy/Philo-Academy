K-5 Program Curriculum

Our K-5 curriculum introduces young learners to classic literature and timeless stories, fostering a love for reading, moral understanding, and critical thinking.

Kindergarten

  • Mother Goose Rhymes (selected rhymes, e.g., “Humpty Dumpty,” “Jack and Jill”) – Short, memorable verses for recitation and joy.
  • The Three Billy Goats Gruff (Paul Galdone version) – A tale of bravery and problem-solving.
  • Goldilocks and the Three Bears – A classic story of curiosity and consequences.
  • The Gingerbread Man (Karen Schmidt version) – A fun tale of adventure and cunning.
  • The Little Red Hen (Paul Galdone version) – Emphasizes the value of hard work and responsibility.
  • Jack and the Beanstalk (Steven Kellogg retelling) – Adventure with themes of courage and greed.

1st Grade

  • Mother Goose Rhymes (selected rhymes, e.g., “Humpty Dumpty,” “Jack and Jill”) – Short, memorable verses for recitation and joy.
  • The Three Billy Goats Gruff (Paul Galdone version) – A tale of bravery and problem-solving.
  • Goldilocks and the Three Bears – A classic story of curiosity and consequences.
  • The Gingerbread Man (Karen Schmidt version) – A fun tale of adventure and cunning.
  • The Little Red Hen (Paul Galdone version) – Emphasizes the value of hard work and responsibility.
  • Jack and the Beanstalk (Steven Kellogg retelling) – Adventure with themes of courage and greed.

2nd Grade

  • D’Aulaires’ Book of Greek Myths (selected stories, e.g., “Theseus and the Minotaur,” “Daedalus and Icarus”) – Vivid retellings with rich illustrations.
  • The Golden Fleece (Padraic Colum retelling) – Jason’s quest for heroism and teamwork.
  • Persephone and the Pomegranate Seeds (Kris Waldherr version) – Explains seasons, explores family ties.
  • King Midas and the Golden Touch (Charlotte Craft retelling) – Warns against greed, visually engaging.
  • Arachne the Weaver (from Favorite Greek Myths, Mary Pope Osborne) – Pride and consequences.
  • Pandora’s Box (Jean Warren retelling) – Curiosity and hope, sparks discussion.
  • Norse Myths – Introduction to Norse mythology and heroic tales.
  • Eastern Myths – Exploration of cultural stories from Eastern traditions.

3rd Grade

  • Charlotte’s Web (E.B. White) – Friendship, sacrifice, and life cycles.
  • The Velveteen Rabbit (Margery Williams) – Love, authenticity, and becoming “real.”
  • The Tale of Peter Rabbit (Beatrix Potter) – Disobedience and consequences, rich language.
  • The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe (C.S. Lewis) – Good vs. evil, redemption (abridged for pacing if needed).
  • Pinocchio (Carlo Collodi, simplified retelling) – Honesty and personal growth.
  • The Princess and the Goblin (George MacDonald, adapted version) – Courage, faith, and adventure in a magical world.

4th Grade

The 4th-grade curriculum includes a structured reading schedule over 36 weeks, focusing on historical and literary texts:

Weeks Title Pages Pages/Day
Weeks 1–2 The Courage of Sarah Noble 60 pages ~6 pages/day
Weeks 3–5 The Sign of the Beaver 135 pages ~9 pages/day
Weeks 6–9 The Fighting Ground 160 pages ~8 pages/day
Week 10 Paul Revere’s Ride 10 pages ~2 pages/day
Weeks 11–12 Rip Van Winkle 30 pages ~3 pages/day
Weeks 13–14 The Legend of Sleepy Hollow 40 pages ~4 pages/day
Weeks 15–16 American Tall Tales 60 pages ~6 pages/day
Weeks 17–22 Little House on the Prairie 250 pages ~8–9 pages/day
Weeks 23–24 Sarah, Plain and Tall 60 pages ~6 pages/day
Weeks 25–30 Light reading or catch-up (e.g., revisit Little House chapters) - ~2–3 pages/day if needed
Weeks 31–36 Buffer for review, essays (e.g., “Was Jonathan brave?”), history units (e.g., colonial life), or activities (e.g., tall tale storytelling) - -

5th Grade

  • Little Women – Family, growth, and resilience in 19th-century America.
  • Tom Sawyer – Adventure, mischief, and moral lessons in a small-town setting.
  • The Wonderful Wizard of Oz – Courage, friendship, and self-discovery in a fantastical world.
  • Pollyanna – Optimism and the power of a positive outlook.
  • Where the Red Fern Grows – Loyalty, love, and loss through a boy’s bond with his dogs.
  • Roll of Thunder, Hear My Cry – Racial injustice and family strength in the 1930s South.
  • Bridge to Terabithia – Friendship, imagination, and coping with loss.
